Lipman, David J. 88 May 24, 1926 Dec. 06, 2014 David John Lipman was born May 24, 1926, in Seattle, to Burton Lipman and Miriam (Shemanski) Lipman. He died peacefully Dec. 6, 2014, at his Portland home, of complications of age, with members of his loving family by his side. David was a businessman who co-founded and operated a chain of retail stores in Oregon, Washington, California and Hawaii known as Mark's Hallmark. David was the grandson of a successful merchant, Joseph Shemanski, who was born in Sluzewo, Poland, in 1869, and arrived in Portland in 1888. Shemanski, along with his brothers, co-founded Eastern Columbia Outfitters, which operated department stores in Seattle, Portland, San Francisco and Los Angeles. David, who was then four years old, and his cousin, Joseph Fulop, were present when the cornerstone of the Eastern Columbia store was laid at S.W. 10th Avenue and Washington Street in Portland, where the Eastern name remains embedded in the sidewalk. Throughout his life, David took inspiration from his grandfather. In 1930, David moved with his family from Seattle to Portland. He was active in scouting and became an Eagle Scout. David attended Lincoln High School and then graduated from Columbia Preparatory School in 1944. He served briefly in the U.S. Marines Corps before attending the University of Washington. He graduated from UW in 1948, with a degree in business administration. While at UW, he was a member of Sigma Alpha Mu, one of the two Jewish fraternities on campus. In 1948, David married Leslie Goldberg, whom he had met at UW. Shortly after they married, he and Leslie moved to Oakland, Calif., where David worked as a buyer at Kahn's Department Store. Then, after three years in Oakland, they moved to Leslie's hometown of Aberdeen, Wash. While in Aberdeen, David was a partner in Goldberg Furniture, a business owned and operated by Leslie's family. David and Leslie had three daughters, Cynthia, Loree and Midge, all of whom were born in Aberdeen in 1952, 1953 and 1955, respectively. In 1956, the family moved to Portland. On returning to Portland, David was in a commercial finance business and later was a co-owner of Associated Tires, along with E.O. "Al" Sinnard. In 1965, David and Al took up an opportunity to acquire a Hallmark store in Spokane. They named their new enterprise Mark's Hallmark. From the first store in Spokane, David, Al and their many dedicated managers and employees oversaw the expansion of Mark's Hallmark to 35 stores in four states. Over the years, David maintained a close relationship with the Hall family in Kansas City, whose company licensed the Hallmark name and provided greeting cards, gift wrap and other core merchandise for the stores. David loved the business and worked hard at it, including over 25 buying trips to Asia to acquire gift items to complement the Hallmark and other domestic goods sold in the stores. When he reached age 70, David and Al completed the sale of the business to an ESOP for the benefit of their employees. Several years after David's first marriage ended in divorce, he was introduced by mutual friends to Beverly (Backer) Wolfstone of Seattle. They were married by Rabbi Raphael Levine Dec. 30, 1968, a snowy night in Seattle, with David's daughters and Bev's two children, Jeff and Nancy, in attendance. Their blended family became and remains very close. David and Bev spent over 40 happy years together. They enjoyed traveling both for business and pleasure, visiting many destinations in the U.S., Mexico, Asia and Europe. Sho- rtly after their marriage, they built a house in Sunriver, where they spent a great deal of time with family and friends both in the winter for skiing and in the summer for tennis, golf and other outdoor activities. A recurrent highlight was the annual ski trip taken by David, Bev and their children and spouses to Sun Valley, Idaho. Later in life, David and Bev acquired a home in Palm Desert, Calif., where they spent the winter months. David specifically asked that a brief history of Bev's life appear here as well as his own. Bev was born May 2, 1931, in Seattle, to James J. Backer and Rose (Mondschein) Backer. She graduated from Garfield High School in Seattle and then attended the University of Washington. In 1951, Bev married Donald L. Wolfstone. Their marriage ended in divorce in 1967. Bev and Don had two children, Jeff and Nancy, born in 1952 and 1957, respectively. Over the years, David and Bev hosted countless family dinners and other events. Bev was a great hostess and cook, and always made sure that every detail was properly attended to. She was interested in style and fashion and was always impeccably dressed. Though it was of course not true, family members affectionately have been known to say that the stock of Nordstrom's took a dip the day she passed away. She has been missed by all, especially David, since her passing May 1, 2009. David had a strong connection with his Jewish heritage, and over the years maintained a very close affiliation with Congregation Beth Israel in Portland. He was elected to the board of Beth Israel and served as congregational president from 1990 to 1992. For 46 years, coterminous with the rabbinate of Rabbi Emanuel Rose, David served as the shofar blower during High Holy Day services. By his example, David encouraged his children and grandchildren to carry on their Jewish heritage. David is survived by his sister, Joan Lindholm of Palo Alto, Calif. Bev is survived by her sister, Carolyn Schwartz; and brother, Fred Backer, both of whom reside in the Seattle area. David and Bev are survived by their children, Loree Sakai (Ken), Midge Cone (Roger), Jeff Wolfstone (Lynn) and Nancy Schlesinger; grandchildren, Sam Aaron, David Sakai, Andrew Sakai, Ali Sakai, Miriam Lipman-Hopkins, Anna Cone, David Cone, Mark Wolfstone, Annie Wolfstone, Katie Schlesinger, Max Schlesinger and Amy Schlesinger; and great-granddaughter, Adeline Cameron. David and Bev were predeceased by Cynthia (Lipman) Aaron. David and Bev were members of the Multnomah Athletic Club, Tualatin Country Club and Desert Falls Country Club. A public memorial service will be held at noon Sunday, Dec. 21, 2014, at Congregation Beth Israel, 1931 N.W. Flanders St., in Portland. The family is grateful for the care provided by David's doctors, nurses and hospice providers in his final years, and particularly wish to acknowledge Lina Chacon. Donations in David's memory may be sent to Congregation Beth Israel.
Published in The Oregonian from Dec. 9 to Dec. 19, 2014
