参加Krzysztof Matyjaszewski教授学术报告会(2015.5.4)

报告题目：Macromolecular Engineering by Taming Free Radicals

报告人：Krzysztof Matyjaszewski教授

主持人：章明秋教授

时间：2015年5月4日（星期一）上午9:30

地点：丰盛堂芙兰学术中心A403

Krzysztof Matyjaszewski教授简介：

    Krzysztof (Kris) Matyjaszewski, Ph.D., professor in the department of chemistry at the Mellon College of Science, Carnegie Mellon University, is an internationally recognized polymer chemist who is highly regarded for his vision, his leadership in education and his many collaborative research efforts that have yielded significant innovations in polymer chemistry.He is best known for the discovery of atom radical transfer polymerization (ATRP), a novel method of polymer synthesis that has revolutionized the way macromolecules are made. He is a member of the U.S. National Academy of Engineering, a foreign member ofRussianAcademyof Sciences and also a foreign member ofPolishAcademyof Sciences.

     In 1985, he joined Carnegie Mellon, where he founded and currently directs the Center for Macromolecular Engineering. In 1998, he was appointed the J.C. Warner Professor of Natural Sciences. In 2004 he was named a University Professor, the highest distinction faculty can achieve at Carnegie Mellon. He has mentoredover 100 postdoctoral fellows and 100 graduate students since joining Carnegie Mellon. He has authored 17 books, 84 book chapters and 875 peer-reviewed scientific papers. His work has been cited in the scientific literature more than 65,000 times, making him one of the most cited chemists in the world. He has received numerous awards for his work, including 2011 Wolf Prize in Chemistry and 2009 Presidential Green Chemistry Challenge Award.
